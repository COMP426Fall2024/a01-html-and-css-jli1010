Electra

The Basics

Electra is a character in Greek mythology who appears frequently in tragedies. She is the main character in two Greek tragedies (plays): Electra by Sophocles and Electra by Euripides. Both plays were written partially to respond to Aeschylus' Agamemnon.

Backstory

A vase depicting Electra, Orestes, and Hermes at Agamemnon's tomb circa 380-370 BC. From Wikipedia.

In Greek mythology, Electra was the daughter of King Agamemnon and Queen Clytemnestra of Mycenae, and had two sisters and a brother: Iphigenia, Chrysothemis, and Orestes. The general story is that before the Trojan War, Agamemnon had sacrified Iphigenia to the gods in order to get a favorable wind to set sail to Troy. When he returned, Clytemnestra and her lover, Aegisthus, killed him in revenge in order to make Aegisthus king.

Orestes, Clytemnestra and Agamemnon's son, was not in Mycenae at the time (the reason differs between versions) and was therefore spared from also being killed, as he was a legitimate challenger for the throne. He returned several years later to avenge his father's death.

Both tragedies begin at this point. Electra's role differs between the two versions, but ultimately assists Orestes in killing Clytemnestra and Aegisthus.

Fun (?) fact: the Electra complex is named after her.

Relationship Table

Electra
  • Daughter of Agamemnon and Clytemnestra
  • Siblings: Iphigenia, Chrysothemis, Orestes
  • Wants revenge for Agamemnon's murder
Orestes
  • Son of Agamemnon and Clytemnestra
  • Siblings: Electra, Iphigenia, Chrysothemis
  • Wants revenge for Agamemnon's murder and to reclaim the throne
Clytemnestra
  • Queen of Mycenae, formerly married to King Agamemnon, now in a relationship with Aegisthus
  • Children: Electra, Iphigenia, Chrysothemis, Orestes
  • Murdered Agamemnon when he returned from the Trojan War for sacrificing their daughter, Iphigenia
Agamemnon
  • Deceased King of Mycenae, sacrified his daughter Iphigenia in order to depart to fight in the Trojan War
  • Murdered by Clytemnestra and Aegisthus when he returned from the war
  • Children: Electra, Iphigenia, Chrysothemis, Orestes
